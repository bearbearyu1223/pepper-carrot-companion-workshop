Hereva's calendar runs on thirteen months of four weeks of seven days each. The months are named First, Second, Third, and so on; an earlier set of more imaginative names was retired after a tax-collection error swapped Thistlebury for Sisalbury, and the Royal Collection Agency now insists on brief, unambiguous names. The seven days of the week align with the six magical schools plus a shared seventh.

The school-days are named alphabetically. Azarday is Chaosah's day, named for the French word for hazard; it functions like a Sunday and is when most contests and events occur (the first potion contest in Komona was held on Azarday). Babkaday is Magmah's day, named for the eastern European sweet bread. Cetoday belongs to Aquah, named for the primordial sea deity. Donday is Zombiah's day, named for the Celtic lord of the dead. Eggday is Hippiah's day, the egg being the symbol of life. Fookuday is Ah's day, named for the Japanese god Fukurokuju. The seventh day is Zero's Day, written as 0x; it is a rest day that celebrates all the schools and the non-magical creatures of Hereva alike. The convention for naming all seven days together is 0xABCDEF, because ABCDEF0x looked wrong to the Hereva Institute of Standards and Measures.

Days have twenty-four moons, parallel to twenty-four hours. The twelve-hour clock uses PinkMoon for post-meridiem hours and AirMoon for ante-meridiem, while the twenty-four-hour clock just numbers the moons.
