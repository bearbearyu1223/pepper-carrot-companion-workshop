The magic of Hereva flows in a substance called Rea, which fills the world so completely that any practitioner can find the resources to cast spells, but channeling them well requires training. Six schools, called covens, organize that training. They arrange on a figure called the Impossible Triangle, divided between two great branches of magic: Chaos and Evolutions. Chaos contains a single school, Chaosah, which sits at the base of the triangle and is considered the magic that flows beneath every other.

The Evolutions branch contains five schools. Hippiah sits at the top-middle and teaches plants, creatures, and all living things; it is the most open school, sharing diluted forms of its magic with farmers across Hereva. Magmah is at the middle-right, teaching cooking, baking, alchemy, and rare metals, deeply intertwined with Komona's wealth and catering tradition. Aquah sits at the middle-left, teaching water, wind, clouds, and the abyss, reclusive since the Great War. Zombiah occupies the top-right, teaching animation and reanimation, centered in Qualicity and its clockwork industry. Ah holds the top-left, teaching the after-life, ghosts, and parallel worlds; its red-robed adepts wander Hereva guided by spirits.

The schools coexisted for centuries before the Great War, with only Chaosah making serious trouble through its pranks. After the war Ah brokered treaties with every other school that survive today. Wasabi of Ah reframed the Impossible Triangle as a Unity Tree with Ah at the apex, which the other schools accept to varying degrees. Chaosah considers the Unity Tree a piece of propaganda; Komonan scholars have noted the lopsided structure as well.
