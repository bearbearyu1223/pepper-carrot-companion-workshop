Chaosah is feared less for any single spell than for its philosophy. It is the school of time, gravity, particle physics, and underground divine forces, and its practitioners value practical magic over flashy displays. A true witch of Chaosah is mean, commands fear and obedience, holds influence over the powerful, does not use magic for daily chores, and treats a wanted poster as the sign of a good reputation. Chaosah witches keep to closed quarters and resent sharing secrets with outsiders.

The school's signature working is the Chaosah Black Hole, discovered by the founder Chicory while tinkering with chaos magic one night. Chaosah witches also summon demons from a dimension outside Hereva, with Pepper learning the rite from the book Incantations for Demons of Chaosah. The three named demons, Hornuk, Eyeük, and Spidük, can be brought through only briefly. Chaosah summoning was one of the things that eventually drew Ah into the Great War, and the right to summon demons today is technically restricted by the treaty Ah keeps in its magic lock-box.

Chaosah has rituals that the other schools find baffling. Failed potions and spells are buried in the ground, both to reclaim some of the Rea spent and as a deterrent to wasteful experimentation. The practice traces back to Chicory and persists today, with the consequence that the soil near a Chaosah house is often more dangerous than the witches living above it. The school venerates the sacred mountain of Tenebrum, which may or may not actually exist on Hereva itself; outsiders who blunder onto it are returned to the world with a note signed by Thyme. The wisdom relayed through Pepper includes Cayenne's advice that a good dark stare is worth a dozen curses and Cumin's that the edible plants are ready-made potions against hunger.
