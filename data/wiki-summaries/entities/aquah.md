Aquah is the school of water, wind, clouds, and the abyss, sitting at the middle-left of the Impossible Triangle as a major school. Little is known about Aquah by outsiders because the chief requirement to attend its courses is the ability to breathe underwater. Aquah is content to keep to itself, communing among its members and the denizens of the sea, and those who try and fail to meet the school's standards tend to be washed ashore for someone else to find.

Aquah traces its origins to an unnamed mermaid called "The First One", whose curiosity about her underwater world led her to experiments with heat, currents, vortexes, and finally the weather of the surface. Her students grew the school until it could influence weather across Hereva. When the spells caused damage to land-dwellers, Aquah apologized and pulled back. Some land-folk asked to learn the magic; those who could learn to breathe and function underwater were welcomed, though such admissions remain rare today.

Aquah started the Great War with Magmah over weather patterns and lake-draining. As part of the treaty Magmah renamed Aquah "Wharrgablah" for ten years as punishment. After the war Aquah became reclusive and withdrew from the other schools, declining even to attend the treaty ratifications at Ah's temples; the school sent its terse, polite regrets and ratified in absentia. Aquah's favorable performance at the second magic contest in Komona, where it sent Spirulina, has loosened some of the old tensions.

The School of Aquah is a series of communal underwater caves in the deepest parts of Hereva's waters, lit by phosphorescent moss. Most lessons are taught via telepathy, though Spirulina has unearthed a cache of written spells that is reviving forgotten parts of the curriculum. Most students come from within Aquah society; The Great War sharply reduced the influx of land-dwellers.
