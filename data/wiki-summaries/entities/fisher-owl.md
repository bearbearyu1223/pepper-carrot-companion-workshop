The Fisher Owl looks like a regular owl of the Squirrel's End forest until it encounters a fishing pole. The presence of a pole triggers an immediate bond, and the owl uses the pole to capture its prey, often baiting a hook with cheese to lure mice. Where the owls acquire the tiny poles is one of the open questions in Hereva ecology; the current theory is that poles pass down from generation to generation, though some speculate that someone or something is busily making them. Fisher Owls have also been spotted in frozen places, which raises the further question of where they obtain the tiny ice-drills.
